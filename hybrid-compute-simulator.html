National Infrastructure in the Age of Intelligent Systems
"A country need not invent every component of the future of computing to shape it."
A country does not need to invent every component of the future of computing in order to shape it — but by building the right infrastructure, it can become the place where that future is realised. The Compute Shift
The Convergence of AI, HPC and Quantum Systems
"Computing is no longer just about machines — it is becoming infrastructure."
The future of computing is described as a race to build better machines — faster processors, larger models, more powerful quantum systems. But something more fundamental is taking shape beneath that narrative. Computing is no longer just about machines; it is becoming infrastructure. High performance computing systems, AI accelerators, and quantum processors are being brought together into unified platforms, with each playing a different role and together creating a system more capable than any one technology alone — this is the hybrid compute era. The book explores that transition, examining how hybrid computing systems are structured, how they can be built, and how they may evolve into platforms that support industries across the world.

Capital in the Shadows
How Private Equity Shapes the Modern World
"Private equity is the quiet force that reshapes industries, realigns incentives, and decides which companies survive."
The book begins with history, situating private equity within centuries of capital pooling, risk sharing, and active ownership. From there, it moves into the architecture of modern private equity: the strategies, deal structures, financial concepts, and operating playbooks that define the industry today. The narrative then shifts to context — how private equity behaves in different geographies, how it evolved in India, and how specific case studies illuminate both success and failure. Later chapters examine the perspective of limited partners and the human dimension of careers, culture, and decision-making inside firms. Throughout, recurring themes emerge: the importance of governance, the fragility of alignment, the tension between financial engineering and real value creation, and the necessity of humility in an industry that deals with uncertainty as its daily raw material.
